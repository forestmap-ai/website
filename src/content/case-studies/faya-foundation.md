## The challenge

Principe Island - a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve off the coast of West Africa - is home to extraordinary biodiversity, including potentially undiscovered plant species. The Faya Foundation, established to protect the island's ecosystems while supporting local livelihoods through a "Natural Dividend" model, needed a comprehensive tree map to establish a baseline for conservation efforts.

The challenge was significant: Principe's forests are dense, tropical, and highly biodiverse, with over 110 documented tree species across just ~16 km of forested area. Prior data coverage was sparse. Field surveys had identified at least six plant species previously unrecorded on the island, suggesting the true diversity was even greater than known. Any mapping approach needed to work in a data-sparse, high-endemism environment where off-the-shelf remote sensing products would fail.

## What forestmap.ai delivered

forestmap.ai conducted tree mapping and species inventory work using remote sensing data tailored to the island's unique conditions. The approach combined:

- High-resolution aerial imagery for individual tree crown detection
- Species-level classification adapted to the island's diverse and endemic flora
- Integration with ground-truth data from botanical field surveys

The result was a spatially explicit tree inventory that gives the Faya Foundation a baseline understanding of what grows where across the island's forest cover.

## The outcome

The tree map provides the Faya Foundation with the ecological baseline it needs to:

- Monitor forest health and composition changes over time
- Demonstrate measurable conservation outcomes to donors and partners
- Support the "Natural Dividend" model, which links economic rewards to ecological responsibility
- Identify priority areas for protection based on species rarity and endemism

## Why it matters

This engagement shows forestmap.ai working at the opposite end of the scale from the Wildlife Works project - a small island rather than a continental jurisdiction - but with the same core approach: turning complex remote sensing data into an ecological inventory that drives real conservation decisions. It also demonstrates the ability to work in environments where generic products fail and bespoke ecological expertise is essential.
